# Шукайпес — Partner Update: June 6 – July 8, 2026

*A plain-language summary of everything that happened in the product over the
last month, written for partners. No technical background needed.*

## The short version

Over the past month the app went from "a working prototype that felt a bit
rough" to "a polished, atmospheric game you'd actually want to open every
day." Roughly **280 improvements** were shipped in four big waves:

1. **Telegram became a full entry point** — people can now report a lost pet
   simply by messaging our Telegram bot, and those reports appear on the live
   map automatically.
2. **The app was translated into Ukrainian and completely redesigned** —
   every screen was rebuilt to feel smooth, modern, and native to the phone.
3. **The dog got a personality upgrade** — the AI companion is now funnier,
   more memorable, and speaks Ukrainian by default.
4. **The city came alive visually and socially** — a beautiful new 3D view of
   Kyiv with fog, sunlight and day/night cycles, plus the first "multiplayer"
   feature: you can now see other walkers' dogs on the map and greet them.

The month closed with a full internal review of where the product stands and
a written roadmap for the pilot launch.

## What the app is (a reminder)

Шукайпес is a lost-pet search network disguised as a gentle walking game.
You walk around real Kyiv with a pixel-art AI dog companion, collect treats,
chat with the dog, and discover partner locations. Meanwhile, real lost-pet
reports from around the city appear on your map — so every player walking
their route becomes an extra pair of eyes in the search. It runs in the
browser on any phone (no app-store download needed) and inside Telegram.

## Week by week

### Early June (June 6–9): polish and cost control

A short tune-up: small fixes to how things look and behave on iPhones, a
calmer color mood for the "sniffing" search feature, and an important
behind-the-scenes change that **sharply cut our Google Maps costs** by
reusing place data across all users instead of paying for the same lookup
again and again.

### June 19–20: Telegram becomes a front door

This was a big strategic step. We built our own Telegram bot, which means:

- **Anyone can report a lost pet by simply messaging the bot** — no need to
  install anything or join a group.
- The bot also **listens to lost-pet posts in Telegram groups** and pulls
  them into the app's live map automatically.
- It understands messy, real-world Ukrainian messages — including slang words
  for "dog," different word forms, and neighborhood names ("біля Оболоні")
  which it matches against a built-in directory of Kyiv places.
- Tapping a link in Telegram opens the app **directly at the pin** of the
  pet in question.
- Old or low-quality reports are cleaned off the map automatically every day.

We also rebalanced the game's progression: there are now 50 levels and more
varied ways to earn points, so the game stays rewarding for longer.

### June 20–21: Ukrainian first

The entire app — every screen, every button, the map's street names, and the
companion dog itself — was translated so that **Ukrainian is the default
language**, with an English toggle available in the profile. The dog's voice
was given a literary Ukrainian flavor inspired by the writer Valerian
Pidmohylny.

### June 21–24: the great redesign

Four intense days rebuilding how the app looks and feels:

- Lost-pet listings became **swipeable card decks** (think Tinder-style
  cards) instead of plain lists — much more engaging to browse.
- Every list of places and pets is now **sorted by how close it is to you**.
- A consistent design language was established: unified sizes, spacing,
  rounded corners and typography across the whole app, so everything feels
  like one product rather than a collection of screens.
- Chat with the dog got comfortable, modern message bubbles.
- The bottom navigation became a **floating pill** that sits elegantly over
  the map.
- Every tappable element now gives a small satisfying "pop" so the app feels
  responsive and alive.

### June 24–28: teaching new users, and a funnier dog

- A **hints system** was built: gentle one-time tips that appear at the right
  moment ("long-press the map to sniff," "swipe the cards," "here's what the
  meters mean") and never nag. They wait politely until you're not busy.
- The AI companion got a serious personality upgrade: we switched its chat
  brain to our **most capable AI model**, taught it real comedic technique,
  gave it **memory for running jokes** that build up across days, and tuned
  it to answer in short, punchy replies rather than walls of text. The vibe
  we aimed for: a witty stranger you meet in the park.
- Lost-pet detail screens were redesigned, and pet photos coming in from
  Telegram now load reliably forever (previously the links could expire).

### June 29–30: feeling like a real app

Fixes so that when people **add the app to their home screen**, it fills the
whole display edge-to-edge like a native app. Collecting paws and bones on
the map was made more game-like, with a satisfying reward moment on pickup.

### June 30 – July 2: the city comes alive

The most visible change of the month. The map was transformed into a
**3D game world**:

- Kyiv's buildings now rise in 3D with soft morning/day/evening/night
  lighting, warm sunlight, drifting mist on the horizon, and gentle ground
  shadows — the city looks like a cozy game environment, not a utility map.
- On older phones that can't handle the 3D view, the app **automatically
  falls back** to the classic map, so nobody is left out.

And the first **social feature** shipped:

- You can now **see other people's dogs walking on the map** around you (up
  to 8 km), and send them a friendly "poke" — their phone gives a little
  vibration in response (now working on iPhones too, which normally block
  this).
- To make the city feel populated from day one, **30 computer-controlled
  dogs** walk the streets with natural, believable movement.

### July 3–4: stability and the road to pilot

The month closed with grown-up housekeeping:

- Automatic **quality checks now block any broken code** from reaching the
  live app.
- Three internal documents were written: a full description of how the
  system is built, an honest **audit of known weaknesses** (with a
  prioritized fix list), and a **pilot roadmap** — where the product stands
  and what decisions are needed to launch a real pilot in Kyiv.

## Where things stand today

**What's live and working:** the walking game with the AI companion, the 3D
city view, multiplayer presence with pokes, Telegram reporting of lost pets,
the Ukrainian-first interface — all of it is running in production right now.

**What the roadmap says, honestly:** the *game* part is genuinely playable
and beautiful. The *lost-pet* part — the actual mission — is built
end-to-end but hasn't yet been validated with real search cases at scale.
The audit's conclusion is that the path to pilot is less about new
engineering and more about **decisions, trust-and-safety groundwork, and
testing the report-to-found loop with real data**. That is the recommended
focus for the coming weeks.
