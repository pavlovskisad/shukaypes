# Шукайпес — Partner Update: June 6 – July 8, 2026

*A plain-language summary of the work done in the product over the last
month, written for partners. No technical background needed.*

## The short version

Over the past month roughly **280 improvements** were shipped, in four main
areas:

1. **Telegram became a full entry point** — people can now report a lost pet
   by messaging our Telegram bot, and those reports appear on the live map
   automatically.
2. **The app was translated into Ukrainian and redesigned** — the interface
   was reworked screen by screen for consistency and ease of use on phones.
3. **The AI companion was upgraded** — the dog now runs on a stronger AI
   model, holds memory across conversations, and speaks Ukrainian by
   default.
4. **The map gained a 3D city view and the first social feature** — other
   walkers' dogs are now visible on the map, with a simple greeting
   interaction.

The month closed with a full internal review of where the product stands and
a written roadmap for the pilot launch.

## What the app is (a reminder)

Шукайпес is a lost-pet search network built into a walking game. Users walk
around real Kyiv with a pixel-art AI dog companion, collect items, chat with
the dog, and discover partner locations. Real lost-pet reports from around
the city appear on the map, so each active user becomes an extra pair of
eyes in a search. It runs in the browser on any phone (no app-store download
needed) and inside Telegram.

## Week by week

### Early June (June 6–9): fixes and cost control

Small corrections to display and behavior on iPhones, adjustments to the
"sniffing" search feature, and a change that **significantly reduced our
Google Maps costs** by reusing place data across all users instead of paying
for the same lookup repeatedly.

### June 19–20: Telegram intake

A significant step for the core mission. We built our own Telegram bot:

- **Anyone can report a lost pet by messaging the bot directly** — no
  installation, no group membership required.
- The bot also **monitors lost-pet posts in Telegram groups** and adds them
  to the app's live map automatically.
- It handles real-world Ukrainian messages — colloquial words for "dog,"
  different word forms, and neighborhood references, which it matches
  against a built-in directory of Kyiv places.
- Tapping a link in Telegram opens the app **directly at the pin** of the
  pet in question.
- Outdated or low-quality reports are removed from the map automatically
  every day.

The game's progression system was also rebalanced: 50 levels and a wider set
of ways to earn points, so engagement holds up over a longer period.

### June 20–21: Ukrainian first

The entire app — every screen, every button, the map's street names, and
the companion dog itself — now **defaults to Ukrainian**, with an English
toggle in the profile.

### June 21–24: interface redesign

Four days of rework across the interface:

- Lost-pet listings became **swipeable card decks** instead of plain lists.
- Lists of places and pets are now **sorted by distance to the user**.
- A single design system was established — unified sizes, spacing, corner
  radii and typography — so the app reads as one product rather than a set
  of separate screens.
- Chat and navigation were restyled to current mobile conventions, and
  tappable elements received uniform touch feedback.

### June 24–28: onboarding and companion behavior

- A **hints system** was built: one-time tips shown at the relevant moment
  ("long-press the map to sniff," "swipe the cards," what the meters mean).
  Hints wait until the user is not mid-action and never repeat.
- The AI companion's chat was moved to our **most capable AI model**. Its
  replies were tuned to be shorter and more varied, and it now keeps
  **memory of recurring jokes and topics across sessions**, so conversations
  build over time instead of resetting.
- Lost-pet detail screens were reworked, and pet photos arriving from
  Telegram now load reliably long-term (previously the links could expire).

### June 29–30: installed-app behavior

Fixes so that when users **add the app to their home screen**, it uses the
full display like a native app. Item collection on the map was given clearer
feedback on pickup.

### June 30 – July 2: 3D city view and presence

The most visible change of the month. The map was rebuilt as a **3D view of
Kyiv**: buildings in three dimensions, lighting that follows the time of day
(morning/day/evening/night), atmospheric haze at the horizon, and ground
shadows. On phones that can't handle the 3D view, the app **automatically
falls back** to the standard map, so no device is excluded.

The first **social feature** also shipped:

- Users can now **see other people's dogs on the map** within an 8 km
  radius and send them a "poke" — the recipient's phone vibrates in
  response (this now works on iPhones as well, which normally restrict it).
- To keep the map from looking empty at low user counts, **30
  computer-controlled dogs** walk the streets with plausible movement
  patterns.

### July 3–4: stability and the road to pilot

The month closed with process work:

- Automatic **quality checks now block defective code** from reaching the
  live app.
- Three internal documents were written: a description of how the system is
  built, an **audit of known weaknesses** with a prioritized fix list, and a
  **pilot roadmap** — where the product stands and what decisions are needed
  to launch a real pilot in Kyiv.

## Where things stand today

**Live and working:** the walking game with the AI companion, the 3D city
view, presence and pokes, Telegram reporting of lost pets, the
Ukrainian-first interface — all running in production.

**What the roadmap says:** the *game* side is functional and presentable.
The *lost-pet* side — the actual mission — is built end-to-end but has not
yet been validated with real search cases at scale. The audit's conclusion
is that the path to pilot is less about new engineering and more about
**decisions, trust-and-safety groundwork, and testing the report-to-found
loop with real data**. That is the recommended focus for the coming weeks.
